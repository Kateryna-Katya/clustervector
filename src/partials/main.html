Cluster Vector

How It Works

  • Survey the Grid

    Each level opens with scattered color-matched clusters sitting at rest on a dark field — your job is to read their positions before touching anything.

  • Draw Your Vectors

    Drag between two same-colored nodes to create a directional link; the angle and length of your draw determines how the clusters travel when released.

  • Trigger the Merge

    Hit go and watch everything move at once. When you nail it, all the pieces come together into one blob and you didn't waste any moves.

Features

Geometry That Matters

The angles aren't just decoration - mess up by even 30 degrees and you'll watch your perfect solution completely fall apart.

Obstacle Grids

Later levels throw in walls and weird gravity stuff, so now you're trying to bank shots off multiple surfaces like some kind of physics pool game.

Move Economy

Every level has a par count; beating it earns a second star, so going back doesn't feel like you're just doing the same thing over.

Procedural Puzzle Lab

There's this sandbox mode that keeps making new puzzles based
on how well you're doing, so you'll never run out of stuff to solve.

FAQ

  • How much spatial math do I actually need?

    Nah, you just pick it up as you go. Don't need to be a math whiz or anything. Geometry helps, but you can get pretty far just going with your gut.

  • Is there a time limit on moves?

    Never. Every level is untimed; the only pressure is the move-count par if you're chasing stars.

  • Can I undo a bad vector draw?

    You can clear and redraw any vector before triggering the release — once you launch, the physics run to completion.

  • What happens after the 80+ levels?

    The Puzzle Lab generates fresh boards indefinitely, and a weekly challenge board posts a new community puzzle every Monday.
